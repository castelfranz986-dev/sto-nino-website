🔬 STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)

STEM is for students who love exploring how things work and solving problems using science and math. They study subjects like Calculus, Physics, Biology, Chemistry, and Robotics. Students in this strand practice critical thinking and experiment with real-world problems.

Examples: Designing a small robot, conducting experiments, analyzing data for a science project.

Best for: Future engineers, doctors, architects, data analysts, and IT specialists.

Goal: To help students become problem solvers and innovators in science and technology.
