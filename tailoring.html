Tailoring (Home Economics)

✂️ Tailoring is a hands-on Technical-Vocational strand that teaches students how to design, create, and produce garments. Students learn taking measurements, drafting patterns, choosing fabrics, and using sewing machines safely and efficiently.
Examples: Making school uniforms, casual wear, or creating a suit from scratch.
Best for: Future fashion designers, tailors, and garment production managers.
Outcome: Students can earn a TESDA National Certificate (NC II), enabling them to work in the garment industry or start their own tailoring shop after high school.
