ADMISSIONS AND ENROLLMENT

Sto. Niño National High School provides quality education for senior high school students with academic and technical-vocational tracks, fostering excellence, discipline, and engagement in school life.

SENIOR HIGH SCHOOL ADMISSION & ENROLLMENT PROCESS

Sto. Niño National High School

AVAILABLE STRANDS

- STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)
- HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)
- Dressmaking
- Aquaculture

I. SUBMISSION OF REQUIREMENTS (ALL STRANDS)

All incoming Grade 11 applicants must submit the following:

Requirements:
1. Photocopy of Grade 10 Report Card (SF 9)

Grade Qualifications:

STEM Applicants
• Final grades from Grade 7 to Grade 10 in Mathematics, Science, and English must be 85% and above
• Final grades in other subjects must be 80% and above
• General Weighted Average (GWA) from Grade 7 to Grade 10 must be 80% and above

HUMSS, Dressmaking, and Aquaculture Applicants
• General Weighted Average (GWA) must be 80% and above

Submission schedules and venues will be announced through official school platforms.

II. ADMISSION TEST (STEM APPLICANTS ONLY)

STEM applicants are required to take a qualifying admission test.

Important Reminders:
• Examinees may bring pencil, eraser, and ball pen only
• Examinees will be accommodated per batch
• The list of qualified exam takers will be posted on official school platforms
• Confirmation may also be sent via text message

Note: There is NO admission test for HUMSS, Dressmaking, and Aquaculture applicants.

III. PERSONAL INTERVIEW (STEM APPLICANTS ONLY)

• All qualified STEM applicants shall undergo a panel interview to validate their personal essays and academic readiness.
• Interview schedules and venues will be announced through official school platforms.

Note: There is NO interview for HUMSS, Dressmaking, and Aquaculture applicants.

IV. ENROLLMENT (ALL STRANDS)

The list of official enrollees will be posted on:

• School Bulletin Board
• Senior High School Department – Official Facebook Page

Enrollment Requirements:
1. Original Grade 10 Report Card (SF 9)
2. Original Certificate of Good Moral Character (for transferees)
3. Photocopy of PSA / NSO Birth Certificate

Enrollment schedules will be announced through official school platforms.

STEM ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

Grade 10 GWA – 30%
Admission Test – 50%
Interview – 20%
Total – 100%

FREQUENTLY ASK QUESTIONS (FAQs)

1. If I don't qualify for STEM, can I shift to HUMSS or TVL?

Yes. Students who do not meet the STEM assessment cutoff are encouraged to enroll in HUMSS, Dressmaking, or Aquaculture, provided they meet the general 80% GWA requirement and slots are still available.

2. I am a transferee from another province. Can I still apply?

Yes. As long as you provide your Original Certificate of Good Moral Character and your PSA Birth Certificate, you follow the same admission process as local students.

3. Do I need to buy my own sewing machine for Dressmaking or tools for Aquaculture?

No. The school provides the necessary laboratory equipment and facilities for technical-vocational strands. However, students may be asked to provide their own basic consumable materials (like fabric for specific projects or personal protective gear).

4. What happens if I miss the STEM Admission Test schedule?

Testing is done by batch. If you miss your schedule due to an emergency, you must immediately contact the Senior High School Coordinator (Mrs. Jessebeth C. Sales) to see if a make-up date is possible.

5. Is there a "No Fail" policy for the STEM Interview?

The interview is not a "pass/fail" exam but a way to measure your academic readiness and interest. It accounts for 20% of your total score. The goal is to ensure you are placed in a strand where you can truly succeed.

6. Can I change my strand after the first semester?

Shifting is generally discouraged because Senior High School subjects are highly specialized from day one. Any request for shifting is subject to the principal’s approval and the availability of slots in the new strand.

7. Where can I find the official results if I don’t have a Facebook account?

While results are posted on the Official Facebook Page, the definitive list is always posted physically on the School Bulletin Board located near the Senior High School Department office.

Quick Tips for Applicants:

Double Check Grades: Ensure your SF9 (Report Card) is clear and readable before submitting.

Be On Time: For STEM applicants, arriving 30 minutes before your batch schedule is highly recommended.

Dress Code: When coming for an interview or submission, please wear a neat and appropriate outfit (school uniform or smart casual).
